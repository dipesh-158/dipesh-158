## Research

### Knee Joint Torque Modeling for Wearable Robotics
Graduate Research Assistant – BEARCATs Lab, University of Cincinnati

Developing subject-specific models to predict knee joint torque from lower-limb kinematics for assistive exoskeletons (with applications in cerebral palsy rehabilitation).

Approach:
- Gaussian Process Regression (GPR)
- Parametric (spring–damper) modeling
- Semi-parametric hybrid models
- Comparative study of parametric vs non-parametric methods

Goal:
Accurate torque prediction with minimal calibration strides, enabling real-time integration into wearable robot controllers.
